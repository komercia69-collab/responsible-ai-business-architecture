# Decision Log: Commercial Phase 0 Deferral

**Status:** Decision Log Entry  
**Decision status:** Accepted by Human Owner  
**Canonical effect:** Commercial track deferred  
**Date:** 2026-05-26  
**Human Owner:** Oleksandr Shuliak  

> Multi-AI agreement is not approval.  
> Commercial content generation is not business approval.  
> Final architectural and commercial approval belongs to the Human Owner.

## Decision

Human Owner defers immediate implementation of the proposed Phase 0 commercial track and RABA Compliance Profile.

RABA will not create an active `commercial/` directory or publish a commercial pilot offer at this stage.

Commercial concepts are preserved as deferred backlog material for possible future product development.

## Context

A prior commercial package proposed a parallel commercial track built on the open-source RABA core, including a pilot offer, positioning statement, integration candidates and revenue sustainability model.

Subsequent review identified risks:

- premature legal or compliance expectations;
- Product Liability and EU AI Act over-claim risk;
- scope creep away from the open-source responsibility architecture core;
- possible confusion between discussion drafts and active commercial offer;
- risk that AI-generated commercial material could be mistaken for Human Owner business approval.

## Rationale

The Human Owner chose to focus the current phase on:

- stabilizing the open-source RABA core;
- strengthening regulatory mapping and governance drafts;
- clarifying framework boundaries;
- avoiding premature commercial, legal or pilot commitments;
- preserving commercial ideas without activating them.

## Deferred Items

The following ideas are deferred, not rejected:

- RABA Compliance Profile;
- pilot offer template;
- commercial positioning statement;
- integration candidates list;
- revenue sustainability model;
- future assessment service around EU AI Act readiness.

## Active Restrictions

At this stage RABA must not:

- create an active `commercial/` working tree;
- publish a pilot offer externally;
- advertise a live compliance product;
- claim that RABA guarantees EU AI Act compliance;
- initiate a client pilot without separate Human Owner confirmation;
- make pricing, referral, partnership, liability or IP commitments.

## Future Reconsideration

The commercial track may be reconsidered only after:

1. the open-source governance core is more stable;
2. regulatory mapping drafts are reviewed;
3. framework boundaries are clearly documented;
4. legal/compliance review is available for any external offer;
5. the Human Owner explicitly reopens the commercial track.

## RABA Principle Demonstrated

This decision is an example of RABA governing its own development.

AI systems proposed a plausible commercial direction. Review surfaced risks. The Human Owner exercised final authority and changed the project course.

The system therefore avoided an AI consensus tunnel: generated quality did not become automatic approval.
