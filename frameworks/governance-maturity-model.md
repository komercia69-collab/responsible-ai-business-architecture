# Governance Maturity Model

## Responsible AI Business Architecture

A practical maturity framework for organizations moving from experimental AI adoption toward governable AI-native operations.

---

# Core Principle

> Governance maturity must scale faster than autonomy.

---

# Purpose

This model helps organizations evaluate:

- operational controllability;
- governance readiness;
- escalation capability;
- AI deployment maturity;
- governance observability;
- runtime accountability.

It is intended for:

- executive teams;
- enterprise architects;
- AI governance initiatives;
- AI transformation programs;
- pilot readiness reviews;
- consultants and system integrators.

---

# Level 0 — AI Chaos

## Characteristics

- uncontrolled prompting;
- unmanaged AI experimentation;
- shadow AI usage;
- no governance ownership;
- no observability;
- no escalation logic.

## Typical Risks

- prompt injection exposure;
- accidental data leakage;
- inconsistent outputs;
- hidden operational influence;
- unclear responsibility.

## Organizational Pattern

AI is used opportunistically without deployment governance.

---

# Level 1 — Assisted Operations

## Characteristics

- isolated AI usage;
- human review present;
- limited automation;
- simple approval flows;
- basic policy awareness.

## Typical Controls

- manual approval;
- restricted access;
- limited external integrations.

## Organizational Pattern

AI acts as a support layer, not an operational actor.

---

# Level 2 — Governed Workflows

## Characteristics

- AI-enabled workflows;
- defined permissions;
- audit trails;
- escalation paths;
- governance checkpoints;
- connector reviews.

## Typical Controls

- prompt governance;
- MCP permission mapping;
- approval gates;
- workflow monitoring.

## Organizational Pattern

AI becomes operationally relevant but remains supervised.

---

# Level 3 — Governable Autonomy

## Characteristics

- semi-autonomous AI operations;
- runtime observability;
- governance telemetry;
- corrective governance;
- drift detection;
- owner-level dashboards.

## Typical Controls

- stop-switches;
- escalation engines;
- rollback capability;
- corrective governance layers;
- human override systems.

## Organizational Pattern

AI systems operate with bounded autonomy under governance constraints.

---

# Level 4 — AI-Native Governance

## Characteristics

- governance nervous system;
- enterprise-wide observability;
- adaptive governance controls;
- continuous governance telemetry;
- operational accountability architecture;
- AI-native governance operations.

## Typical Controls

- integrated governance fabric;
- runtime policy orchestration;
- dynamic escalation systems;
- governance-aware workflows;
- enterprise governance dashboards.

## Organizational Pattern

Governance becomes a continuous operational capability.

---

# Maturity Comparison

| Capability | Level 0 | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI visibility | None | Partial | Structured | Continuous | Enterprise-wide |
| Human oversight | Informal | Manual | Defined | Integrated | Systemic |
| Auditability | None | Limited | Present | Operational | Continuous |
| MCP governance | None | Minimal | Managed | Controlled | Adaptive |
| Drift detection | None | None | Partial | Active | Continuous |
| Corrective governance | None | Manual | Limited | Active | Integrated |
| Executive visibility | None | Limited | Partial | Dashboarded | Real-time |

---

# Strategic Insight

Many organizations increase AI capability faster than governance maturity.

This creates:

- invisible operational instability;
- governance blindness;
- unmanaged autonomy;
- escalating organizational risk.

---

# Strategic Goal

The objective is not maximum autonomy.

The objective is:

> governable autonomy.

---

# Recommended Next Step

Organizations should assess:

1. current governance maturity;
2. operational controllability gaps;
3. connector and MCP exposure;
4. escalation readiness;
5. observability coverage;
6. corrective governance capability.

---

# Responsible AI Business Architecture

> AI capability creates speed.
> Governance maturity preserves stability.
