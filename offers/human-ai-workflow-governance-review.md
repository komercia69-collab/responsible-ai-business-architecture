# Human-AI Workflow Governance Review

> A focused review of one AI-assisted workflow before automation becomes operational risk.

## One-Sentence Offer

I help organizations review one AI-assisted workflow, identify where AI output becomes operational action, and define the human approval, logging and escalation points needed before autonomy increases.

---

## Who This Is For

This offer is for teams that are already using, testing or planning AI assistants, copilots or AI agents inside real business workflows.

It is especially relevant for:

- business owners and operational leaders;
- CTOs and heads of AI;
- consultants and enterprise architects;
- governance, legal, compliance and security roles;
- teams using AI to draft, recommend, update, classify, trigger or execute workflow steps.

---

## The Problem

Many organizations introduce AI into workflows before they clearly know:

- where AI is only assisting;
- where AI output becomes operational action;
- who approves AI-supported decisions;
- what must be logged;
- what can be reversed;
- when escalation is required;
- who remains accountable if something goes wrong.

The review helps make these boundaries visible before automation expands.

---

## What We Review

One real or planned AI-assisted workflow.

Examples:

- customer support triage;
- CRM updates;
- internal email drafting;
- document review;
- lead qualification;
- AI-assisted reporting;
- scheduling or operational coordination;
- agentic workflow with tools or API access.

The workflow can be simplified or anonymized.

Sensitive data is not required for the first review.

---

## Format

Suggested first format:

| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| Session 1 | Understand the workflow and planned AI role |
| Analysis | Map AI action boundaries and governance risks |
| Session 2 | Review findings and refine approval / escalation points |
| Optional Session 3 | Discuss pilot path or next implementation step |

Typical scope:

- one workflow;
- two to three sessions;
- practical written output;
- fixed scope;
- no production deployment.

---

## Output

The client receives a short practical review package.

Possible outputs include:

- workflow summary;
- AI Action Boundary Map;
- AI Governance Gateway Map;
- human approval points;
- escalation points;
- logging and evidence requirements;
- rollback / correction considerations;
- main governance risks;
- recommended next step.

The output should be understandable by business, technical and governance stakeholders.

---

## What This Is Not

This review is not:

- legal advice;
- a compliance certification;
- a security audit;
- a full AI safety evaluation;
- a production implementation;
- a guarantee that AI risk can be eliminated.

It is a focused workflow governance review designed to make responsibility, approval and operational risk visible.

---

## What the Client Provides

A client should provide a simple description of the workflow:

| Input | Example |
|---|---|
| Workflow | Customer support ticket triage |
| Current AI use | AI drafts response and suggests category |
| Planned AI use | AI may update CRM field or trigger escalation |
| People involved | Support agent, team lead, customer success manager |
| Main concern | Wrong categorization or unapproved customer communication |
| Desired outcome | Clear boundary before increasing automation |

---

## Example Review Question

```text
In this workflow, where does AI output stop being advice and start becoming operational action?
```

That question often reveals the most important governance boundary.
