# RABA Small Pilot Offer

**Status:** Draft — External Feedback / Pilot Exploration Offer  
**Purpose:** Test RABA against real or planned AI-assisted workflows  
**Audience:** AI consultants, workflow owners, governance teams, product builders, compliance-oriented organizations  
**Canonical status:** Non-canonical offer draft. This document does not define accepted RABA architecture.  
**Boundary:** This offer does not validate RABA, create a commercial commitment, or imply a live pilot relationship.

## Short Description

RABA offers a small structured feedback and pilot-exploration review for one AI-assisted workflow.

The goal is not to sell software access or request system integration.

The goal is to help a team understand where responsibility, approval, escalation, and auditability should exist before AI-assisted execution becomes operational.

## Core Principle

> AI may be probabilistic.  
> Responsibility must not be.

## What the Pilot Reviews

The pilot focuses on one real or planned AI-assisted workflow, for example:

- AI-assisted decision preparation;
- AI-generated recommendations;
- human approval of AI-prepared outputs;
- AI-assisted customer, operational, legal, HR, medical, financial, or compliance workflows;
- internal automation where execution boundaries are unclear.

The workflow does not need to be fully implemented.

A written description, mockup, process outline, or anonymized example is enough.

## What RABA Provides

For one selected workflow, RABA prepares:

### 1. Action Boundary Map

Identification of where AI preparation ends and where human-owned decision or execution begins.

### 2. Governance Gateway Routing Assessment

Classification of whether the workflow should follow a fast path, governed preparation route, escalation path, or locked approval path.

### 3. Responsibility Event Stream Outline

A structured outline of what should be recorded for auditability:

- who prepared the output;
- who reviewed it;
- what evidence was viewed;
- what approval state existed;
- what boundary was crossed;
- what human authorization was required;
- what decision or escalation was recorded.

### 4. Decision Log / Governance Record Template

A lightweight template showing how key decisions should be preserved.

### 5. Drift and Rubber-Stamp Risk Review

Identification of risks such as:

- approval becoming a button click;
- human review becoming passive;
- AI consensus being mistaken for approval;
- fast-path metrics becoming performance targets;
- governance thresholds being relaxed without policy integrity review;
- AI-prepared material becoming so polished that human review remains formal but not practical.

### 6. Two-Page Pilot Report

A concise final report with:

- main risks;
- recommended governance controls;
- open questions;
- suggested next implementation step.

## What Is Not Required

This pilot does not require:

- access to production systems;
- source code access;
- customer data;
- confidential documents;
- API integration;
- vendor lock-in;
- long procurement process.

An anonymized workflow description is sufficient.

## Possible Formats

### No-Fee Pilot Exploration Review

For selected early reviewers or strategic collaborators.

Purpose:

- test RABA concepts against realistic workflow examples;
- receive structured feedback;
- test whether the framework is useful in real workflows;
- improve public documentation.

### Paid Small Pilot Review

For organizations or consultants who want a structured review deliverable.

Possible scope:

- one workflow;
- one governance assessment;
- one two-page report;
- one feedback session.

The paid version is not positioned as software implementation.

It is a structured responsibility and governance review.

## Suggested Input From Participant

To begin, the participant provides:

1. A short description of the AI-assisted workflow.
2. The intended AI role.
3. The intended human role.
4. What action or decision may happen after AI output.
5. What could go wrong if the AI output is accepted too easily.
6. Whether the action is reversible, partially reversible, or difficult to reverse.
7. Whether human approval currently requires evidence review or only an approval action.

## Output

The participant receives:

- Action Boundary Map;
- Governance Gateway recommendation;
- Responsibility Event Stream outline;
- Decision Log / Governance Record Template;
- drift and rubber-stamp risk notes;
- two-page pilot report.

## Positioning

RABA does not replace legal, compliance, security, procurement, or domain-specific professional review.

RABA helps teams see where responsibility must be designed into AI-assisted workflows before automation pressure turns preparation into silent execution.

## Important Governance Boundary

This pilot may produce recommendations, draft controls, and review structures.

It does not approve production deployment, legal compliance, risk acceptance, or organizational policy changes.

Final approval remains with the responsible human owner or organization.

Multi-AI agreement, consultant agreement, or tool-generated confidence must not be treated as sufficient approval.

## Key Message

Governed speed is useful only when responsibility remains visible, reviewable, and human-owned.

AI can prepare.  
AI can recommend.  
AI can challenge.

But responsibility must remain assigned, recorded, and governed.
