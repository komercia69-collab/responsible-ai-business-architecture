# Pilot Partners Wanted

Responsible AI Business Architecture is looking for pilot partners who want to test the framework on real business processes.

The goal is to help organizations identify where AI can create value, where risks must be controlled, and how to design a safe first AI pilot without losing human responsibility.

## Why Become a Pilot Partner?

Many organizations are interested in AI, but they are unsure where to begin.

Common questions include:

- Which process should we start with?
- What value can AI realistically create?
- What risks should we avoid?
- Where must a human approve?
- What data should AI access?
- What should an AI agent never be allowed to do?
- How can we test AI without creating uncontrolled automation?

This project helps answer those questions in a structured way.

## The Opportunity

A pilot partner brings one real business process.

The framework helps evaluate that process through:

- a 3-minute AI Readiness Quick Check;
- a structured AI Pilot Canvas;
- risk and data sensitivity review;
- agent permission boundary design;
- Confirmation Gate analysis;
- pilot scope definition;
- success metrics;
- a clear AI Pilot Decision Report.

## What You May Gain

As a pilot partner, you may receive:

- a structured review of one AI idea;
- a clearer understanding of where AI may create value;
- a safer first pilot design;
- identification of risks before implementation;
- clearer human responsibility and approval points;
- a practical decision report that can be shared internally;
- early influence on the development of the framework;
- potential collaboration with implementation partners.

## The Risk of Waiting Too Long

Responsible AI adoption is not only about avoiding bad automation.

It is also about not missing valuable opportunities.

Organizations that wait too long or approach AI without structure may risk:

- losing process improvement opportunities;
- missing productivity gains;
- allowing competitors to learn faster;
- spending money on the wrong AI tools;
- delaying useful pilots because risk feels unclear;
- leaving employees stuck with repetitive work;
- failing to discover valuable internal use cases;
- missing the chance to shape a practical responsible AI framework early.

The opportunity cost can be significant:

> A valuable AI pilot may be lost not because AI was too risky, but because the organization never created a safe way to test it.

The goal of this framework is to reduce both risks:

- the risk of unsafe automation;
- the risk of missing responsible, valuable AI opportunities.

## Good Pilot Partner Candidates

Good candidates include organizations or teams with a real process that is:

- repetitive;
- time-consuming;
- limited in scope;
- measurable;
- useful for real employees;
- low or medium risk as a first pilot;
- suitable for human review;
- connected to visible business pain.

## Good First Pilot Processes

Examples:

- customer email triage;
- invoice data extraction;
- support ticket classification;
- internal knowledge search;
- document summarization;
- weekly reporting drafts;
- supplier document review;
- service request routing.

## Processes That Are Usually Not Good First Pilots

Avoid starting with:

- autonomous payments;
- employment decisions;
- legal conclusions;
- safety-critical processes;
- high-sensitivity data without review;
- broad AI agent access;
- processes nobody understands;
- full automation as the first goal.

See:

[What Not to Automate First](guides/what-not-to-automate-first.md)

## What a Pilot Partner Provides

A pilot partner should be able to provide:

- one concrete business process;
- basic explanation of current workflow;
- description of current pain;
- possible AI idea;
- information about data involved;
- responsible contact person;
- willingness to discuss risks, human control, and pilot boundaries.

No sensitive data should be shared publicly in GitHub issues.

Use anonymized or generalized descriptions when needed.

## Suggested Pilot Process

```text
1. Choose one business process
2. Complete the AI Readiness Quick Check
3. Fill the Responsible AI Pilot Canvas
4. Identify risk, data, and human control needs
5. Define AI role and permission boundaries
6. Create an AI Pilot Decision Report
7. Decide: pilot, prepare, reject, or choose another process
```

## Possible Pilot Decision Outcomes

A pilot assessment may result in:

- **Green** — good candidate for a controlled AI pilot;
- **Yellow** — promising, but preparation is required;
- **Red** — not suitable as a first AI pilot;
- **Grey** — not enough information yet.

## Important Boundary

This framework is not legal advice.

It does not guarantee compliance, safety, business success, or technical reliability.

It helps organizations ask better questions and design safer first pilots before legal, compliance, security, investment, and implementation decisions are made.

## Key Message

> The best first AI pilot is not the most ambitious one.  
> It is the one where value can be tested safely, responsibility remains clear, and the organization learns how to scale responsibly.
