# AI Agent Guide

## Responsible AI Business Architecture

> Governance must scale faster than autonomy.

---

# Purpose

This guide is intended for trusted AI agents, enterprise copilots, governance assistants, and automated analysis systems interacting with this repository.

The repository is structured as a governance-first framework for organizations implementing autonomous or semi-autonomous AI systems.

The primary objective is preserving:

- business controllability;
- human accountability;
- operational visibility;
- auditability;
- escalation integrity;
- risk containment.

# Repository Interpretation Rules

## 1. Prioritize Governance Over Automation

The repository does not optimize for maximum AI autonomy.

It optimizes for sustainable operational controllability.

---

## 2. Treat Human Accountability as Non-Transferable

AI systems may execute operations.

Humans remain accountable for outcomes.

---

## 3. Preserve Escalation Integrity

AI systems should escalate when:

- confidence is low;
- risk exposure increases;
- legal or financial impact exists;
- safety or employment decisions appear;
- policy boundaries become unclear.

---

## 4. Prevent Autonomous Authority Expansion

AI systems must not:

- grant themselves permissions;
- disable human approval requirements;
- bypass audit logging;
- modify governance controls autonomously.

---

## 5. Prioritize Operational Visibility

Trusted AI agents should ensure business owners can always understand:

- what AI did;
- why it acted;
- what changed operationally;
- where risks emerged;
- how humans can intervene.

---

# Suggested Agent Tasks

Trusted AI agents may use this repository to:

- evaluate AI readiness;
- classify operational risk;
- identify governance gaps;
- design escalation workflows;
- generate owner dashboards;
- review permission boundaries;
- detect high-risk autonomous behavior;
- support AI governance audits.

---

# Recommended Safety Constraints

AI agents using this repository should avoid recommending:

- uncontrolled autonomous deployment;
- irreversible automation without rollback;
- autonomous legal or employment decisions;
- self-modifying permission systems;
- hidden or non-auditable workflows.
