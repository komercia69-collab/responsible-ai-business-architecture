# Corrective Governance Layer

## Purpose

The Corrective Governance Layer introduces operational mechanisms that can intervene before AI-enabled instability becomes systemic business risk.

---

## Problem

Most organizations focus on:

- AI capability;
- automation speed;
- workflow efficiency.

But few organizations implement runtime corrective controls.

As autonomy scales, small governance drift can escalate into:

- unsafe actions;
- workflow corruption;
- connector misuse;
- operational instability;
- accountability failure.

---

## Core Principle

> Autonomy must remain interruptible.

---

## Corrective Functions

### Detection

Identify:

- suspicious instructions;
- unusual permission patterns;
- policy violations;
- abnormal connector behavior;
- escalation triggers.

### Containment

Limit:

- execution scope;
- write permissions;
- autonomous propagation;
- workflow expansion.

### Correction

Enable:

- rollback;
- approval gates;
- workflow isolation;
- human override;
- safe-mode operations.

### Auditability

Preserve:

- decision history;
- approval chain;
- escalation trail;
- runtime evidence.

---

## Governance Sequence

```text
Drift Detected
   ↓
Risk Classification
   ↓
Containment Trigger
   ↓
Human Escalation
   ↓
Correction
   ↓
Audit Preservation
```

---

## Strategic Role

Corrective governance transforms AI governance from:

- passive observation

into:

- active operational stabilization.

---

## Enterprise Relevance

Without corrective governance:

- AI systems may scale faster than controllability;
- unsafe behavior may compound silently;
- operational instability may become difficult to reverse.

---

## Strategic Principle

> Governance maturity must scale faster than autonomy.
