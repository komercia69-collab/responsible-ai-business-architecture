# Preliminary Decision Report

## Use Case

Autonomous AI-based customer refund handling.

---

# Preliminary Governance Decision

## RED — Do Not Start Yet

The proposed pilot should not proceed as an autonomous operational implementation.

---

# Main Decision Factors

The proposed AI system combines:

- probabilistic analysis;
- financial influence;
- customer impact;
- operational execution;
- weak reversibility;
- insufficient governance maturity.

The current process lacks:

- mandatory human confirmation;
- sufficiently defined escalation paths;
- strong auditability;
- rollback clarity;
- clear accountability ownership.

---

# Governance Conclusion

The organization is attempting to automate a process that is not yet sufficiently standardized, reversible, reviewable, or governable.

Autonomous execution is therefore considered unsafe at the current maturity level.

---

# Recommended Alternative

Downgrade the pilot to:

## AI-assisted refund recommendation workflow

Under this redesign:

AI may:

- classify complaints;
- summarize customer history;
- suggest policy references;
- estimate refund ranges;
- prepare draft responses.

Humans must:

- approve refunds;
- authorize financial actions;
- manage escalations;
- review customer communication;
- override incorrect recommendations.

---

# Required Governance Improvements Before Reconsideration

Before reconsidering higher AI authority, the organization should:

- standardize refund policy handling;
- define accountable process ownership;
- introduce confirmation gates;
- implement audit logging;
- define rollback procedures;
- formalize escalation rules;
- improve fraud review processes.

---

# Responsible AI Principle

Responsible AI governance must include the ability to say:

> Do not automate this process yet.
