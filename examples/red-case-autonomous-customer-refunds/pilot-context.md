# Pilot Context

## Proposed Use Case

An organization proposes an AI-based autonomous customer refund handling system.

The intended system would:

- read incoming customer complaints;
- classify the complaint category;
- analyze customer history;
- determine refund eligibility;
- calculate refund amount;
- trigger refund workflows;
- send customer-facing communication.

The organization hopes to reduce:

- customer support workload;
- response time;
- operational cost;
- refund handling delays.

---

## Business Environment

Example environment:

- e-commerce platform;
- SaaS provider;
- subscription business;
- digital services company;
- marketplace platform.

The organization experiences:

- high complaint volume;
- pressure to automate customer support;
- inconsistent manual refund handling;
- customer satisfaction concerns.

---

## Current Governance State

The proposed pilot reveals several governance weaknesses:

- refund authority is not clearly documented;
- escalation paths are inconsistent;
- refund exceptions are handled informally;
- auditability requirements are weak;
- rollback procedures are unclear;
- fraud review is partially manual and inconsistent;
- customer communication standards vary across teams.

---

## Proposed AI Authority

The proposed AI system would influence:

- financial actions;
- customer outcomes;
- operational workflow execution;
- customer communication;
- internal escalation flow.

This places the AI system close to a high-risk operational control loop.

---

## Initial Governance Observation

The organization is attempting to automate a process that is not yet sufficiently standardized, governed, measurable, or reversible.

The pilot therefore requires redesign before implementation.
