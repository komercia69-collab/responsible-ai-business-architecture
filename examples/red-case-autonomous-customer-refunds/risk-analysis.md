# Risk Analysis

## Overview

This proposed AI pilot introduces multiple high-risk characteristics because it combines probabilistic AI analysis with operational financial execution.

The system would directly influence:

- customer outcomes;
- financial actions;
- workflow execution;
- escalation decisions;
- customer communication.

---

# Financial Risk

Incorrect AI recommendations may result in:

- unauthorized refunds;
- inconsistent refund decisions;
- cumulative financial leakage;
- policy violations;
- loss of revenue.

The risk increases when refund approval becomes autonomous.

---

# Fraud Exposure

AI systems may be vulnerable to:

- manipulation attempts;
- adversarial customer behavior;
- repeated refund exploitation;
- prompt manipulation;
- social engineering patterns.

Weak human review increases fraud exposure.

---

# Legal and Compliance Risk

The system may create:

- inconsistent contractual handling;
- unequal customer treatment;
- undocumented decision logic;
- unclear accountability;
- auditability gaps.

Customer-facing financial decisions may create legal obligations.

---

# Customer Escalation Risk

Incorrect autonomous refund handling may:

- damage trust;
- increase customer frustration;
- create escalation loops;
- produce inconsistent communication;
- increase complaint complexity.

---

# Accountability Gap

The proposed pilot does not clearly define:

- who owns final refund authority;
- who reviews incorrect decisions;
- who is accountable for AI-generated financial outcomes;
- who manages escalation exceptions.

This creates a governance gap.

---

# Overtrust Risk

Human operators may gradually overtrust AI-generated refund recommendations.

This can reduce:

- independent review quality;
- exception detection;
- fraud awareness;
- operational skepticism.

---

# AI as Control Loop Risk

The proposed system approaches a high-risk AI control loop because it may influence:

- workflow routing;
- customer treatment;
- financial execution;
- escalation behavior;
- operational priorities.

The faster the AI can influence these systems, the stronger the required governance architecture must be.

---

# Core Governance Observation

The system combines probabilistic analysis with irreversible or difficult-to-reverse financial execution.

This creates an unacceptable governance risk for an autonomous pilot.

---

# Recommended Governance Response

Do not deploy the system as an autonomous refund agent.

Instead:

- downgrade the AI role to recommendation support;
- introduce mandatory confirmation gates;
- define rollback procedures;
- improve auditability;
- standardize escalation rules;
- assign accountable human ownership.
