# Example Use Case: Internal Knowledge Assistant

This example shows how the Responsible AI Business Architecture framework can be applied to a low-to-medium risk AI pilot.

## Use Case

An organization wants to introduce an internal AI assistant that helps employees search, summarize, and structure information from internal documents such as policies, procedures, onboarding materials, FAQs, and operational guidelines.

The AI assistant does not make decisions.  
It supports employees by helping them find and understand existing information.

## Why this use case is suitable as a first pilot

This use case is a good starting point because:

- the AI system supports internal users;
- the AI does not directly affect customers, employees, or legal rights;
- human users remain responsible for decisions and actions;
- outputs can be reviewed before use;
- access permissions can be clearly limited;
- auditability can be introduced without heavy system complexity.

## Core principle

AI may help retrieve, summarize, and structure knowledge.

Human users remain responsible for interpretation, communication, and final decisions.
