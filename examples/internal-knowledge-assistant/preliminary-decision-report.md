# Preliminary Decision Report: Internal Knowledge Assistant

## 1. Use case summary

The organization wants to test an internal AI assistant that helps employees search, summarize, and structure information from approved internal knowledge sources.

The AI assistant is intended to support internal knowledge work, not to make decisions.

## 2. Preliminary signal

**Yellow signal**

## 3. Reason for signal

The use case appears suitable for a controlled pilot because:

- it supports internal employees;
- it does not directly make decisions about people;
- human users can review the output;
- permissions can be limited;
- auditability can be introduced at pilot level.

However, the use case is not Green yet because several controls must be clearly defined before launch:

- approved data sources;
- restricted data categories;
- AI permission boundaries;
- source transparency;
- human review checkpoints;
- escalation rules;
- documentation expectations.

## 4. Main benefits

Potential benefits include:

- faster access to internal knowledge;
- reduced time spent searching documents;
- better onboarding support;
- more consistent internal answers;
- easier preparation of summaries and draft notes;
- identification of outdated or conflicting internal documents.

## 5. Main risks

Potential risks include:

- incorrect summaries;
- outdated information;
- missing source references;
- overreliance on AI-generated answers;
- accidental access to restricted documents;
- unclear responsibility for AI-supported conclusions.

## 6. Required controls before pilot launch

Before launch, the organization should define:

- which documents are included;
- which documents are excluded;
- who may use the assistant;
- what the assistant is allowed to do;
- what the assistant must never do;
- when human review is required;
- how outputs should be documented;
- how users can report incorrect or risky outputs.

## 7. Recommended pilot conditions

The pilot should start with:

- a small internal user group;
- non-sensitive knowledge sources;
- clear user instructions;
- manual review of important outputs;
- documented feedback;
- regular review by a responsible process owner.

## 8. Human responsibility statement

The AI assistant may support knowledge retrieval and summarization.

It must not replace human responsibility.

Human users remain responsible for verifying important information, making decisions, communicating conclusions, and escalating unclear or sensitive cases.

## 9. Recommended next step

Proceed with a controlled pilot preparation phase.

Do not proceed to broad deployment until the permission boundary, access controls, human review checkpoints, and documentation expectations are clearly defined.
