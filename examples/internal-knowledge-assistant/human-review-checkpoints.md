# Human Review Checkpoints: Internal Knowledge Assistant

This document defines where human review is required in the pilot.

## 1. Before pilot launch

A responsible human must review:

- approved knowledge sources;
- access permissions;
- excluded document categories;
- risk signals;
- escalation rules;
- communication to pilot users;
- documentation expectations.

## 2. During AI-assisted knowledge search

Human review is required when:

- the AI output is used for an operational decision;
- the answer may affect another person;
- the source document is unclear or outdated;
- the AI gives a confident answer without a clear source;
- the answer combines multiple sources and creates an interpretation;
- there is a conflict between documents.

## 3. Before external communication

AI-generated text must be reviewed by a human before it is sent to:

- customers;
- employees outside the pilot group;
- partners;
- authorities;
- legal representatives;
- external service providers.

## 4. Before process changes

AI-generated recommendations must not directly change business processes.

A responsible process owner must review and approve:

- process changes;
- policy updates;
- new internal instructions;
- automation improvements;
- access rule changes.

## 5. Regular pilot review

The pilot team should periodically review:

- common AI errors;
- unclear outputs;
- escalation cases;
- user feedback;
- access permission problems;
- missing or outdated documents;
- cases where users relied too strongly on AI output.

## 6. Decision ownership

The AI assistant does not own decisions.

Decision ownership remains with:

- the employee using the output;
- the process owner;
- the responsible manager;
- the compliance, privacy, legal, or governance function where applicable.
