# Pilot Context: Internal Knowledge Assistant

## 1. Business process

The pilot focuses on internal knowledge access.

Employees often need to search across different internal documents, guidelines, procedures, onboarding materials, FAQs, and operational instructions.

The current process is slow, fragmented, and dependent on knowing where information is stored.

## 2. Proposed AI support

The organization wants to test an AI assistant that can:

- search internal knowledge sources;
- summarize relevant information;
- structure answers for employees;
- point users to source documents;
- help prepare draft explanations or internal notes.

## 3. Intended users

The intended users are internal employees, such as:

- operations staff;
- support teams;
- onboarding coordinators;
- team leads;
- internal process owners.

## 4. AI system role

The AI system acts as an assistant.

It may support information retrieval, summarization, and structuring.

It must not act as a decision-maker.

## 5. Human responsibility

Human users remain responsible for:

- checking the relevance of AI outputs;
- verifying important information against source documents;
- deciding whether and how to use the information;
- communicating final conclusions to other people;
- escalating unclear or sensitive cases.

## 6. Data involved

The pilot may involve:

- internal policies;
- internal process documents;
- onboarding materials;
- internal FAQs;
- non-confidential operational guidelines.

The pilot should not initially include:

- personal employee records;
- customer personal data;
- confidential legal documents;
- medical information;
- financial decision data;
- sensitive HR files;
- trade secrets without additional controls.

## 7. Main risks

Potential risks include:

- incorrect or outdated summaries;
- missing context;
- overreliance on AI output;
- accidental access to restricted documents;
- unclear responsibility for decisions based on AI-generated answers;
- weak documentation of AI-supported work.

## 8. Pilot goal

The goal of the pilot is to test whether an internal AI assistant can improve access to organizational knowledge while maintaining human responsibility, access control, transparency, and auditability.
