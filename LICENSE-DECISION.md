# License Decision

This project is currently published without a formal open-source or Creative Commons license.

Until a formal license is added, all rights are reserved by the original author.

## Current Status

The materials may be read, discussed, referenced, and reviewed for feedback.

They may not be copied, redistributed, commercialized, or presented as someone else's framework without explicit permission from the author.

This temporary approach is intentional because the project is still in an early conceptual and practical stage.

## Why the License Is Not Final Yet

The project combines several types of material:

- conceptual framework language;
- business architecture patterns;
- consulting-style templates;
- compliance-oriented checklists;
- practical examples;
- possible future diagrams and training materials.

Different parts of the project may require different licensing decisions.

For example:

- some documents may later be shared under a Creative Commons license;
- some templates may be shared for practical reuse with attribution;
- some consulting materials may remain protected;
- some technical examples may use an open-source software license if code is added later.

## Possible Future Options

The author may later choose one of the following directions:

### Option 1 — All Rights Reserved

The project remains publicly readable, but reuse requires explicit permission.

This gives the strongest protection for the original concept during early development.

### Option 2 — Creative Commons Attribution

Some documents may be reusable with attribution to the original author and project.

This can help the framework spread while preserving authorship.

### Option 3 — Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial

Some documents may be reusable for non-commercial education, research, and discussion, but not for commercial consulting or resale without permission.

### Option 4 — Mixed Licensing

Different parts of the repository may use different rules.

For example:

- framework documents: Creative Commons with attribution;
- consulting templates: restricted or non-commercial;
- code examples, if added: open-source software license;
- diagrams and training materials: separate permission rules.

## Current Rule

Until a formal license is published:

> All rights are reserved by the original author.

If you want to reuse, translate, republish, commercialize, or adapt the framework, please request explicit permission first.
