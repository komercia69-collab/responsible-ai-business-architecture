Responsible AI Business Architecture

The Missing Governance Layer in AI Architectures

Every AI system today defines how to route, generate, and respond. None defines who is accountable when the system acts — and what happens when outcomes require review, escalation, or accountability.

6

This is not a criticism of existing AI architecture layers. These layers were simply not designed to carry business accountability. That is a different problem — and a different layer.

Without RABA
AI routes requests by task type. Fast. Autonomous. No accountability trace.
→
With RABA
AI routes by risk level. Accountable party assigned at every step. Escalation path defined.
Without RABA
Memory stores context. No distinction between context and decision record.
→
With RABA
Every decision is logged with: who authorized, what state, what was the responsible party.
Without RABA
Extensions connect to everything. No gate. No boundary. Capability defines scope.
→
With RABA
Action Boundary defines what AI may do without authorization — and what requires human sign-off.
