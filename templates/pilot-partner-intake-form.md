# Pilot Partner Intake Form

A short preparation form for organizations that want to discuss one real business process as a potential responsible AI pilot.

This form is intended to be completed before a [Pilot Partner Call](../offers/pilot-partner-call.md) or [Responsible AI Pilot Discovery Session](../offers/responsible-ai-pilot-discovery-session.md).

> AI may be probabilistic. Responsibility must not be.

## Purpose

The purpose of this form is to help an organization describe one concrete business process clearly enough for structured analysis.

It helps prepare a conversation about:

- business value;
- current workflow pain;
- AI opportunity;
- data and risk;
- human responsibility;
- permission boundaries;
- pilot readiness;
- next steps.

This is not a technical implementation form.

It is a business-process intake form for responsible AI assessment.

## Important Privacy Boundary

Do not include confidential, personal, customer, financial, health, HR, legal, security-sensitive, or proprietary information in public GitHub issues or public documents.

Use generalized or anonymized descriptions where needed.

Examples:

- write “customer email with order question” instead of a real customer message;
- write “invoice PDF” instead of uploading real invoices;
- write “internal knowledge base” instead of naming confidential systems;
- describe data categories instead of sharing raw data.

Notes:

- Do not share raw sensitive data in this form.
- Describe data categories only.
- If unsure, mark “Not sure”.

## 7. Risk and Consequences

What could go wrong if AI makes a mistake?

```text
Possible AI mistake:
Who could be affected?
Could the mistake be detected by a human? Yes / No / Not sure
Could the mistake be corrected or reversed? Yes / No / Not sure
Potential impact: Low / Medium / High / Critical
```

Examples of higher-risk areas:

- payments;
- legal rights;
- employment decisions;
- medical or health-related decisions;
- safety-critical operations;
- sensitive personal data;
- external customer communication without review.

## 8. Human Responsibility and Approval

Where must a person remain responsible?

```text
Who owns the final result today?
Who should review AI output?
Who should approve final action?
Where should AI stop?
Where is a Confirmation Gate needed?
```

A responsible pilot should make clear:

- who checks;
- who approves;
- who can override;
- who is accountable.

## 9. Permission Boundary

```text

AI may not access:
AI may not execute:

```

The basic principle:

> Permissions should be granted from responsibility, not from technical capability.

## Suggested Output After Review

After reviewing this intake form, the next deliverable may be:

- [Responsible AI Pilot Canvas](responsible-ai-pilot-canvas.md)
- [AI Pilot Decision Report](ai-pilot-decision-report.md)

Possible decision outcomes:

- **Green** — good candidate for a controlled AI pilot;
- **Yellow** — promising, but preparation is required;
- **Red** — not suitable as a first AI pilot;
- **Grey** — not enough information yet.

Organizations should involve appropriate legal, compliance, security, technical, and business experts before implementation decisions are made.
