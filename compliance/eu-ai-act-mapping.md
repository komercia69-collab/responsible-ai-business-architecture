- human oversight;

- permission control;

## Human Oversight Principle

A central principle of this framework is:

> AI may support, recommend, summarize, classify, or prepare.  
> Humans remain responsible for approval, escalation, and execution of critical actions.

This principle aligns with the broader governance objective that AI systems should remain subject to meaningful human oversight.

---

## Operational Boundary Principle

The framework distinguishes between:

- informational support;
- workflow influence;
- operational execution;
- autonomous control.

Risk increases significantly when AI systems move from:

- generating information

toward

- influencing processes, permissions, routing, records, or external actions.

This is described within the framework as:

> AI as Control Loop

The stronger the influence, the stronger the governance architecture must become.
