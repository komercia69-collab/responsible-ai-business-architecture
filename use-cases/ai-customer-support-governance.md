# Use Case: AI Customer Support Governance

## Responsible AI Business Architecture

---

# Scenario

A large organization deploys AI copilots and autonomous agents inside customer support operations.

The objective is improving:

- response speed;
- ticket routing;
- issue classification;
- multilingual support;
- operational efficiency.

However, the organization must preserve:

- customer trust;
- escalation integrity;
- operational visibility;
- legal accountability;
- reputational safety.

---

# Operational Risks

Potential risks include:

- hallucinated responses;
- emotionally inappropriate interactions;
- unauthorized compensation offers;
- invisible AI influence on staff;
- escalation bypass;
- customer manipulation;
- audit gaps.

---

# Governance Architecture

## Human Layer

Responsible roles:

- support agents;
- escalation supervisors;
- customer experience lead;
- governance lead.

---

## Governance Layer

Includes:

- escalation policy;
- permission matrix;
- customer risk classification;
- approval thresholds;
- compensation boundaries.

---

## AI Orchestration Layer

AI systems may:

- classify tickets;
- suggest responses;
- prioritize queues;
- summarize conversations;
- detect sentiment.

AI systems may not autonomously:

- admit liability;
- provide legal guarantees;
- issue large compensation;
- bypass escalation procedures.

---

# Escalation Rules

## Escalate Automatically When:

- customer emotional intensity increases;
- legal threats appear;
- financial disputes emerge;
- AI confidence declines;
- sensitive data appears;
- repeated failures occur.

---

# Governance Metrics

Example metrics:

| Metric | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Escalation Success Rate | Validate human intervention |
| Hallucination Incident Rate | Detect unreliable outputs |
| Customer Satisfaction | Measure trust stability |
| Override Frequency | Measure operational correction |
| Governance Drift Score | Detect workflow divergence |

---

# Failure Modes Prevented

This governance model reduces:

- recommendation-execution collapse;
- escalation collapse;
- invisible AI influence;
- audit decay;
- customer trust erosion.

---

# Strategic Interpretation

The objective is not replacing customer support teams.

The objective is creating scalable support systems that remain controllable and trustworthy.

---

# Strategic Principle

Customer-facing AI systems require stronger governance than internal automation systems.
