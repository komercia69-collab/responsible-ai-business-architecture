# Governance Principles

## Responsible AI Business Architecture

A concise set of principles for deployment-level governance of AI agents, MCP/connectors and autonomous workflows.

---

# 1. Governance Must Scale Faster Than Autonomy

AI capability can expand quickly.

Governance capability must expand faster.

---

# 2. Runtime Controllability Matters More Than Static Policy

Static policy is not enough for AI-native operations.

Organizations need runtime mechanisms to observe, intervene and correct.

---

# 3. Trust Is Not a Substitute for Governance

Trusting a tool, vendor or MCP developer does not eliminate runtime governance risk.

Trust boundaries still require observability, permission control and escalation.

---

# 4. Write Actions Require Stronger Controls Than Read Actions

Read actions and write actions have different risk profiles.

Write actions require stronger governance controls, human review and auditability.

---

# 5. Visibility Without Intervention Is Insufficient

Monitoring is not enough.

Governance requires the ability to contain, redirect or stop unsafe operational behavior.

---

# 6. AI Systems Become Governance Problems Once They Become Operational Actors

When AI can use tools, trigger workflows or affect business processes, governance must move from principles to operations.

---

# 7. Governance Observability Is as Important as Technical Observability

Technical logs show system behavior.

Governance telemetry shows whether the system remains controllable, accountable and aligned with owner intent.

---

# 8. MCP / Connectors Expand Enterprise Risk Surfaces

MCP/connectors can increase both business capability and operational risk.

They require explicit permission mapping, trust-boundary review and escalation design.

---

# 9. Corrective Governance Must Exist Before Autonomy Scales

Detection alone is not enough.

Autonomy should scale only when containment, rollback and human override are available.

---

# 10. Human Override Is a Governance Capability

Human override should be designed, tested and visible.

It should not be an informal emergency workaround.

---

# 11. Governance Integrity Includes Repository Practices

Governance claims must match operational reality.

Security posture, incident response, CODEOWNERS and review processes are part of governance integrity.

---

# 12. AI Governance Without Runtime Visibility Becomes Governance Theater

If AI-enabled operations cannot be observed or audited, governance becomes symbolic rather than operational.

---

# 13. Operational Controllability Is a Competitive Advantage

Organizations that scale AI safely will not only move faster.

They will remain more stable, auditable and trusted.

---

# 14. Governance Must Remain Practical, Observable and Actionable

Governance should not stop at principles.

It should produce operating models, dashboards, playbooks, controls and escalation paths.

---

# 15. The Objective Is Not Maximum Autonomy

The objective is governable autonomy.

AI should become more capable only while remaining observable, controllable and accountable.

---

# Core Principle

> AI may be probabilistic. Responsibility must not be.
