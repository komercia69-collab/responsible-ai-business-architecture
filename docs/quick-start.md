# Quick Start Guide

This guide helps you apply the Responsible AI Business Architecture framework to one AI idea in a structured way.

The goal is not to sell automation.

The goal is to decide whether one concrete business process is ready for responsible AI support.

> AI may be probabilistic. Responsibility must not be.

## 1. Choose One Business Process

Start with one specific process, not a broad AI ambition.

Good examples:

- customer email triage;
- invoice data extraction;
- support ticket classification;
- internal knowledge search;
- weekly reporting drafts;
- service request routing.

Avoid starting with broad goals like:

- “use AI everywhere”;
- “automate operations”;
- “replace manual work”;
- “deploy an AI agent across all systems”.

## 2. Complete the Quick Check

Use the [AI Readiness Quick Check](../QUICK-CHECK.md).

Score the process from 0 to 14.

Interpretation:

- **12–14** — possible Green candidate for a controlled pilot;
- **7–11** — Yellow: promising, but preparation is needed;
- **0–6** — Red: probably not ready as a first pilot.

You can also try the browser-based [Responsible AI Pilot Decision Assistant](../demo/index.html).

## 3. Describe the Process

Use the [Pilot Partner Intake Form](../templates/pilot-partner-intake-form.md) if you need a structured preparation step.

Clarify:

- how the process works today;
- what pain AI should reduce;
- what data is involved;
- who owns the final result;
- what could go wrong;
- where human review is required.

## 4. Fill the Pilot Canvas

Use the [Responsible AI Pilot Canvas](../templates/responsible-ai-pilot-canvas.md).

The canvas helps define:

- business objective;
- AI role;
- data and risk;
- human control;
- permission boundary;
- confirmation gate;
- success metrics;
- pilot scope.

## 5. Review Governance Observability

Before allowing AI to influence operational decisions, review:

- where conflicts may appear;
- how escalation works;
- where human confirmation is required;
- which actions must remain blocked;
- how AI influence becomes observable;
- how rollback and correction would work.

Recommended document:

- [Conflict Detection & Governance Observability](conflict-detection-and-governance-observability.md)

## 6. Produce a Decision Report

Use the [AI Pilot Decision Report](../templates/ai-pilot-decision-report.md).

Possible outcomes:

- **Green** — good candidate for a controlled AI pilot;
- **Yellow** — promising, but preparation is required;
- **Red** — not suitable as a first AI pilot;
- **Grey** — not enough information yet.

## 7. Decide the Next Step

Depending on the decision, the next step may be:

- run a limited pilot;
- prepare the process first;
- choose a safer use case;
- collect more information;
- involve legal, compliance, security, architecture, or implementation experts.

## Important Boundary

This framework does not provide legal advice, compliance certification, security certification, or implementation approval.

It helps organizations ask better questions and make clearer decisions before implementation.
