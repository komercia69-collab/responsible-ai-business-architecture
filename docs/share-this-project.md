## Longer Message

```text
I would like to share an open-source project called Responsible AI Business Architecture.

The project addresses a practical problem many organizations are beginning to face: AI is moving from assistance toward operational participation. AI systems can draft, recommend, trigger tools, update records and support decisions, but human responsibility must remain visible and actionable.

The project is not only about AI ethics or policy. It focuses on operational architecture: AI action boundaries, governance gateways, meaningful human approval, decision logs, escalation paths and controlled pilots for one real workflow.

A useful first step is a minimal Human-AI Governance Pilot: select one workflow, map where AI output becomes operational action, identify approval points and define what must be logged or escalated before autonomy increases.

Project:
https://github.com/komercia69-collab/responsible-ai-business-architecture
```

## For Potential Pilot Partners

```text
If your organization is exploring AI in a real business workflow, this project may help structure a safe first pilot.

The pilot does not start with full automation.

It starts with one workflow:
- What is AI allowed to observe, analyze, recommend, draft, trigger or execute?
- Where must AI stop?
- What requires human approval?
- What should be logged?
- What can be reversed?
- Who is accountable?

A good first conversation can begin with a simplified or anonymized workflow description.
```
