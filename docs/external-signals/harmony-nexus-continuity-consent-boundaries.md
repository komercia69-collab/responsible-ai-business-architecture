## Summary

Harmony Nexus appears to work on an adjacent problem space to RABA: continuity, consent boundaries, memory custody, audit receipts, bounded agency, and safe state transitions in long-running AI systems.

This note records the signal as external conceptual input only.

Harmony Nexus is not validation of RABA.

## Why this signal is relevant

RABA focuses on responsibility architecture for AI-assisted business workflows:

- who owns an action;
- where AI must stop;
- when human authorization is required;
- what evidence must be visible;
- what is logged;
- how responsibility remains replayable and reviewable.

Harmony Nexus appears to focus on continuity and custody of long-running AI-system state:

- continuity-safe memory;
- consent-gated access;
- audit receipts;
- lane routing;
- state recovery;
- bounded agency.

The overlap is not identity of architecture. It is adjacency of concerns.

This is adjacent to RABA's concern with human control and responsibility-preserving workflow boundaries.

## Potential RABA learning candidate: shadow-first migration

One potentially useful concept is **shadow-first migration**.

In RABA terms, this could become a future rollout pattern:

> A new AI agent, model, or workflow route should first operate in shadow mode, generate comparable outputs and receipts, and earn trust through review before being allowed to change workflow state or receive expanded permissions.

Possible RABA framing:

```text
new model / agent / route
→ shadow mode
→ comparison against current workflow
→ receipts / evidence
→ human review
→ limited authorization
→ expanded authority only after explicit decision
```

This is only a candidate idea.

It is not accepted RABA canon.
