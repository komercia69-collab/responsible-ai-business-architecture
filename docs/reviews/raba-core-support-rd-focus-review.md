---

## 1. Review question

The review question is:

```text
Does the project visually and structurally distinguish RABA core from supporting materials, experimental tracks, internal governance showcase, and deferred commercial concepts?
```

The concern is not that the project lacks coherence.

The concern is that a new external reader may see many strong adjacent tracks and incorrectly conclude that RABA is “everything about AI governance”.

---

## 2. Core meaning of RABA

The current core should remain focused on operational responsibility architecture:

```text
AI-supported action
→ governed boundary
→ authority / admissibility / responsibility handling
→ Decision Log
→ Responsibility Event Stream / replay
→ accountable speed
```

Short framing:

```text
RABA is about keeping responsibility explicit when AI-supported work becomes consequential action.
```

---

## 3. Core layer

The core layer includes materials that directly define responsibility, governed speed, action boundaries, decision records, responsibility events, and policy integrity.

Candidate core areas include:

- operational responsibility definition;
- responsibility throughput / governed speed;
- speed protection;
- policy integrity;
- responsibility layer for agentic AI;
- approval state model;
- governance gateway;
- Decision Log;
- Responsibility Event Stream;
- framework boundary statements;
- standards mappings that clarify, but do not replace, RABA.

These items directly support the main goal:

```text
Make AI-supported actions accountable, observable, traceable, and governable without killing speed.
```

---

## 4. Supporting layer

The supporting layer helps readers understand, apply, test, or demonstrate the core.

Examples of supporting material types:

- start-here and role-based navigation;
- project summary and value documents;
- self-assessment documents;
- pilot readiness materials;
- partner conversation materials;
- use cases;
- security and threat maps tied to responsibility failures;
- failure patterns;
- starter-kit examples;
- diagrams and demos that prove the core is not only theoretical.

These materials are valuable, but they should not be mistaken for the core definition of RABA.

They translate the core into practical use.

---

## 5. Potential defocus sources

Potential defocus sources are not necessarily wrong.

They may be valuable R&D, internal governance, or future product ideas.

The risk is visual and status-related: if they appear too close to the core, they can make RABA look broader than it is.

Current potential defocus categories:

### 5.1 Experimental research directions

Examples include:

- Governance Nervous System;
- Living Human-AI Operational Interface;
- Personal AI Operational Twin;
- Trusted Decision Intelligence;
- Model-Agent Fit Governance;
- Human-AI Operational Coordination Lattice.

These should be marked as experimental future tracks, not current RABA core.

### 5.2 Extended topology and working notes

Examples include:

- semantic dependency maps;
- responsibility event topology;
- speed / responsibility acceptance notes;
- governed bypass extensions;
- action-class working notes;
- Human Response Window-related working notes.

These may be important R&D inputs, but they should remain visually and status-wise separate from accepted core unless separately canonicalized.

### 5.3 Deferred commercial layer

Commercial concepts, paid offerings, and product packaging should remain separated from the open RABA core unless Human Owner explicitly changes that phase.

### 5.4 Multi-AI operating model

The multi-AI operating model and AI collaboration protocols are strong dogfooding examples.

They should be presented as:

```text
Project-internal governance showcase:
RABA principles applied to RABA development itself.
```

They should not appear as a separate product scope.

---

## 6. Recommended focus controls

Recommended controls:

1. Add a small README focus guard before or near Research Directions.
2. Mark Research Directions as out-of-core experimental future tracks.
3. Mark multi-AI operating model as project-internal governance showcase.
4. Keep commercial concepts visibly deferred.
5. Keep non-canonical notes visually separated from accepted core.
6. Avoid presenting R&D tracks as products or canonical architecture.
7. Avoid large public positioning rewrite until Human Owner confirms.

---

## 7. Suggested reader-facing hierarchy

A clearer external hierarchy could be:

```text
Core RABA Scope
  → responsibility, governed speed, boundaries, gateway, Decision Log, events, policy integrity

Supporting Materials
  → navigation, assessments, pilots, examples, diagrams, mappings

Experimental Future Tracks
  → research directions, advanced topology, operational twins, coordination lattices

Project Internal Governance Showcase
  → multi-AI operating model, collaboration protocol, recommendation triage

Deferred Commercial Concepts
  → commercial backlog and future product packaging
```

This hierarchy keeps the project open to future development without making every direction look like core.
