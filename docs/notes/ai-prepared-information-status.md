# AI-Prepared Information Status

**Status:** Working Note  
**Canonical status:** Non-canonical  
**Related issue:** #160  
**Purpose:** Define an initial RABA inquiry around the status, verification boundary, and responsibility trace of AI-prepared information.  
**Approval status:** Human Owner review required before PR readiness, README linking, public positioning, canonicalization, or merge.

---

## 1. Core problem

AI systems and AI agents increasingly prepare information that may later influence or enter decision processes.

This may include:

- summaries;
- document analysis;
- recommendations;
- risk assessments;
- source comparisons;
- code explanations;
- draft decisions;
- regulatory or technical document preparation;
- agent-prepared action plans.

The risk is that AI-prepared information may be treated as decision-ready too early.

```text
AI-prepared information may be useful,
but usefulness does not make it official.
```

---

## 2. Why status matters

Human-prepared and officially confirmed information often carries a responsibility trace:

- author;
- role;
- organization;
- procedure;
- review;
- signature;
- approval;
- accountability boundary.

AI-prepared information may be valuable, but its status is often unclear.

Without explicit status, the same AI output may be mistaken for:

- a draft;
- a review aid;
- verified evidence;
- an official record;
- a decision basis.

These statuses must not be mixed.

---

## 3. Initial status categories

Possible RABA status categories for AI-prepared information:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Draft | Early AI-prepared material, not reviewed |
| Working note | Useful internal material, not canon |
| Review aid | Material prepared to support human review |
| Analytical support | AI-supported analysis, not official evidence |
| Unverified AI-prepared information | Output not yet checked against sources |
| Human-reviewed AI information | Output reviewed by a responsible human |
| Verified input | Information checked against an approved source or process |
| Officially adopted information | Information accepted through a defined human/organizational process |
| Decision input | Information allowed to influence a decision |
| Evidence / factual material | Information treated as evidence under a defined procedure |
| Canonical record | Accepted RABA record, only after Human Owner approval |
| External / public statement | Material intended for external use, requiring separate approval |

---

## 4. Initial RABA formulas

```text
Useful ≠ official.
Official status ≠ truth.
Truth ≠ responsibility.
Responsibility requires role, procedure, verification boundary, and trace.
```

```text
AI recommendation ≠ decision.
AI confidence ≠ verification.
Multi-agent agreement ≠ validation.
Human approval ≠ responsible authorization.
```

```text
No official decision use without information status.
```

```text
AI may support the decision chain,
but it must not silently become the responsibility chain.
```

---

## 5. Verification boundary

Before AI-prepared information enters an official decision chain, the system or process should clarify:

- What is the information?
- Who or what prepared it?
- Which sources were used?
- Which sources were not checked?
- Has a human reviewed it?
- Who assigned its status?
- Can it be cited?
- Can it be used as a decision input?
- What uncertainty remains?
- What is the responsibility trace?

---

## 6. Decision-chain risk

AI-prepared information can shape a decision before the human approves it.

This may happen when AI:

- selects relevant facts;
- excludes alternatives;
- summarizes evidence;
- frames the issue;
- assigns confidence;
- recommends action;
- prepares execution;
- hides uncertainty through polished language.

In such cases, the human may formally approve the decision while the decision field has already been shaped by AI.

---

## 7. Relation to Human Accountable Release

AI-prepared information status is closely related to Human Accountable Release.

A human release should not be treated as responsible authorization if the human does not know:

- what information status applies;
- what was verified;
- what was not verified;
- what AI shaped;
- what uncertainty remains;
- what consequences may follow;
- what responsibility trace exists.

```text
No accountable release inside an invisible AI-shaped information field.
```

---

## 8. Relation to responsibility-aware AI decision training

Responsible AI adoption may require responsibility-aware humans, not only technically capable AI systems.

Humans and organizations may need to learn that:

- AI output is not automatically a fact;
- AI summaries are not primary sources;
- AI recommendations are not decisions;
- AI confidence is not verification;
- multi-agent agreement is not validation;
- human approval is not always responsible authorization;
- official decision use requires information status and responsibility trace.

This note does not approve a training product, course offer, certification, or commercial program.

## 11. Working summary

AI-prepared information can be useful, but usefulness does not define its status.

Before such information enters an official decision chain, RABA should require explicit status, verification boundary, and responsibility trace.

> AI may prepare information.  
> Humans and organizations must assign status.  
> Official decision use requires responsibility trace.
