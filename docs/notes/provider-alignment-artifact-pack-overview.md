## 4. Why these artifacts belong together

The organizational evals artifact creates useful questions.

The provider / external signals log template creates a safe intake and handling process for any answers.

Together they form a controlled loop:

1. RABA publishes or shares a practical artifact only after Human Owner approval.
2. A practitioner or provider may respond with public or shareable insight.
3. The response is recorded as an external informational signal.
4. The signal is checked for shareability, quotation boundaries, public-use permission, and endorsement risk.
5. The signal may inform future working notes.
6. The signal does not become canon unless separately reviewed and approved by the Human Owner.

This prevents a common governance failure:

> External agreement or attention is mistaken for architectural approval.

---

## 5. Operating principles

### Artifact first

RABA should lead with useful governance artifacts, not requests for recognition.

### People second

RABA may later ask narrow questions to practitioners or researchers, not generic corporate requests.

### Corporations third

Formal provider channels may be used only after artifacts and boundaries are clear.

### Signals always bounded

Every external response remains an external informational signal unless the Human Owner explicitly changes its status.

### No vendor authority

No provider, practitioner, researcher, regulator, standard, or external AI review becomes RABA governance authority.

## 6. Safe use sequence

This sequence is not approved outreach. It is a working control model.

1. Prepare artifact.
2. Review artifact internally.
3. Human Owner decides whether artifact may be shared publicly or externally.
4. Ask one narrow question.
5. Record any response in the Provider / External Signals Log.
6. Mark shareability and citation boundary.
7. Mark what the signal may inform.
8. Mark what the signal does not imply.
9. Link back to any file, issue, PR, decision log, or public material that uses the signal.
10. Request Human Owner decision before public use, canonicalization, or external positioning.
