# AI Readiness Dimensions

## Purpose

Organizations often evaluate AI initiatives mainly from a technical perspective.

Responsible AI Business Architecture expands this view by evaluating not only technical feasibility, but also operational responsibility, governance readiness, and process controllability.

This document introduces four practical readiness dimensions that can support AI pilot evaluation and discovery discussions.

---

## 1. Process Maturity

### Key Question

Is the underlying business process already stable, understandable, and repeatable?

### Positive Signals

- documented workflows;
- defined process owner;
- measurable outcomes;
- existing review procedures;
- escalation paths;
- low operational ambiguity.

### Risk Signals

- chaotic or undefined workflows;
- unclear ownership;
- undocumented exceptions;
- highly inconsistent execution;
- unresolved organizational conflicts.

### Principle

AI should not become a substitute for missing process clarity.

---

## 2. Data Quality and Availability

### Key Question

Is the required information accessible, reliable, and sufficiently structured?

### Positive Signals

- trusted data sources;
- consistent formatting;
- source traceability;
- sufficient historical examples;
- permission-controlled access.

### Risk Signals

- fragmented data;
- unclear source ownership;
- outdated records;
- missing auditability;
- inconsistent terminology;
- uncontrolled sensitive information.

### Principle

Poor data quality creates probabilistic instability.

---

## 3. Technical Infrastructure

### Key Question

Can the organization safely support, monitor, and control AI-supported workflows?

### Positive Signals

- authentication and access control;
- logging and monitoring;
- rollback capability;
- integration stability;
- API governance;
- sandbox or testing environment.

### Risk Signals

- unmanaged integrations;
- missing monitoring;
- weak permission controls;
- lack of incident response;
- unclear deployment ownership;
- uncontrolled automation.

### Principle

The faster AI can influence systems, the stronger the control architecture must be.

---

## 4. Organization and Governance

### Key Question

Can the organization responsibly govern AI-supported decision processes?

### Positive Signals

- accountable process owner;
- review checkpoints;
- escalation procedures;
- human override;
- audit trail expectations;
- cross-functional communication.

### Risk Signals

- unclear responsibility;
- governance gaps;
- overtrust in AI outputs;
- pressure to bypass review;
- lack of approval boundaries;
- undefined incident ownership.

### Principle

AI may assist decisions.
Responsibility must remain human-governed.

---

## Suggested Usage

These dimensions can support:

- AI readiness discussions;
- pilot partner discovery sessions;
- consultant workshops;
- early-stage AI evaluations;
- implementation planning;
- governance discussions.

They are not a certification framework.

They are intended as practical guidance for structured AI pilot preparation.

---

## Relation To Responsible AI Business Architecture

The dimensions support:

- human responsibility;
- operational stability;
- auditability;
- controllability;
- permission governance;
- safe AI pilot scaling.
