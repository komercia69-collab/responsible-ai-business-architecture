# Human–AI Coordination Interface Prototype

> Autonomy may scale. Governability must scale faster.

## Purpose

This document defines a first prototype of an interface system for coordinating highly autonomous AI agents, humans and business owners around shared operational goals.

The goal is not to create a final system.

The goal is to create a first usable coordination model that can evolve faster than agent autonomy.

The prototype is designed around one core question:

> How can humans, business owners and autonomous AI agents remain coordinated, informed and responsible while business processes continuously change?

---

## Core Principle

The interface must not only show what AI agents are doing.

It must show what is happening to the business process itself:

- what is improving;
- what is deteriorating;
- what requires correction;
- what requires human decision;
- where autonomy is increasing;
- where governance is lagging;
- where business development or business recovery is needed.

The system must translate machine-speed operations into human-scale understanding, responsibility and decision-making.

---

## Prototype Name

## Human–AI Coordination Room

The Human–AI Coordination Room is a prototype interface environment that connects:

- human intent;
- business ownership;
- AI agent activity;
- operational process dynamics;
- risk signals;
- governance boundaries;
- improvement statistics;
- corrective actions;
- strategic decisions.

It is not only an AI monitoring dashboard.

It is a responsibility and development interface for human-AI operations.

---

## Users

The prototype has several user perspectives.

### 1. Business Owner

The business owner does not need to see every technical detail.

The business owner needs to understand:

- whether the business process is improving or worsening;
- what requires strategic decision;
- where the organization is gaining value;
- where risk is accumulating;
- what should be developed;
- what should be corrected;
- what should be stopped;
- what needs investment, restructuring or sanitation.

### 2. Operational Manager

The operational manager needs to understand:

- which workflows are active;
- which AI agents are involved;
- where delays or bottlenecks appear;
- which actions require approval;
- where escalation is needed;
- whether human teams are overloaded.

### 3. Governance / Compliance Owner

The governance owner needs to understand:

- whether action boundaries are respected;
- whether approvals are logged;
- whether AI agents act within permission limits;
- where risk thresholds are exceeded;
- which incidents or near misses occurred.

### 4. AI / Technical Team

The technical team needs to understand:

- model behavior;
- tool calls;
- retrieval quality;
- latency;
- failures;
- prompt changes;
- evaluation results;
- technical drift.

### 5. Human Team Members

Human team members need to understand:

- what AI is doing for them;
- what AI needs from them;
- what they remain responsible for;
- where they must review, approve, correct or reject AI output.

## Interface Layers

The first prototype consists of seven interface layers.

```text
1. Business Owner Interface
2. Mission Interface
3. Agent Boundary Interface
4. Live Coordination Interface
5. Governance Gateway Interface
6. Business Process Dynamics Interface
7. Learning and Improvement Interface
```

---

## 1. Business Owner Interface

This is the strategic decision layer.

It gives the owner a compact view of the business process.

It should not overload the owner with raw logs.

It should answer:

- What is developing positively?
- What is deteriorating?
- What needs correction?
- What needs investment or development?
- What needs sanitation or stopping?
- Which AI-driven changes created measurable value?
- Which AI-driven changes created risk or confusion?
- Which decisions are waiting for the owner?

### Key widgets

- Business health summary
- Positive dynamics
- Negative dynamics
- Required owner decisions
- Development recommendations
- Sanitation recommendations
- Risk and accountability summary
- Improvement / deterioration statistics

### Example

```text
Business Process: Customer Support

Positive dynamics:
- First response time improved by 34%.
- Routine ticket resolution improved by 18%.
- Human workload decreased in low-risk cases.

Negative dynamics:
- Escalations increased by 12% in refund-related tickets.
- Compliance review delays increased.
- Customer frustration signals rose in complex cases.

Owner decision needed:
- Approve or reject expansion of AI drafting to medium-risk tickets.
- Decide whether refund workflows need stricter approval rules.
- Invest in better policy retrieval or keep current limits.

Recommendation:
Develop AI support for low-risk workflows.
Correct refund escalation logic.
Do not increase autonomy for legal-risk cases yet.
```

---

## 2. Mission Interface

The Mission Interface defines the shared purpose.

Before autonomous agents act, the system must clarify:

- mission name;
- mission owner;
- business goal;
- success criteria;
- red lines;
- risk tolerance;
- allowed autonomy level;
- required human approvals.

### Core question

> What is the shared task, and what must not be sacrificed while pursuing it?

---

## 3. Agent Boundary Interface

The Agent Boundary Interface defines what each AI agent may and may not do.

Each agent must have:

- role;
- purpose;
- allowed inputs;
- allowed outputs;
- allowed tools;
- forbidden actions;
- autonomy level;
- approval requirements;
- escalation rules;
- accountable human owner.

### Action levels

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Observe | AI may monitor or read information |
| Analyze | AI may identify patterns or produce analysis |
| Recommend | AI may suggest possible actions |
| Draft | AI may prepare text, documents or decisions |
| Trigger | AI may request a workflow action |
| Execute | AI may perform an approved operational action |
| Escalate | AI must involve a human or governance owner |

### Principle

> Analysis is not execution. Recommendation is not decision. Draft is not approval.

---

## 4. Live Coordination Interface

This is the operational room.

It shows:

- active missions;
- active agents;
- active workflows;
- pending approvals;
- blocked actions;
- risk signals;
- human attention required;
- agent conflicts;
- workflow bottlenecks.

The purpose is not to show everything.

The purpose is to show what matters now.

### Core question

> Where does human attention create the highest governance and business value right now?

---

## 5. Governance Gateway Interface

The Governance Gateway controls the transition from AI output to operational action.

It is activated when AI requests actions such as:

- sending external communication;
- updating customer records;
- changing prices;
- approving refunds;
- modifying schedules;
- publishing content;
- triggering workflows;
- changing permissions;
- escalating cases;
- creating binding commitments.

The gateway must answer:

- Is this action allowed?
- Which agent requested it?
- Which human owns it?
- What data was used?
- What is the risk level?
- Does it require approval?
- Can it be reversed?
- Is it logged?
- What happens if it fails?

---

## 6. Business Process Dynamics Interface

This layer is essential for business owners.

It shows not only agent activity, but business process movement.

It tracks:

- process performance;
- quality changes;
- delays;
- error rates;
- customer impact;
- employee workload;
- cost changes;
- risk accumulation;
- governance load;
- improvement velocity;
- deterioration signals.

### Positive dynamics examples

- faster response time;
- fewer manual steps;
- better documentation;
- improved customer satisfaction;
- reduced repetitive workload;
- higher consistency;
- better early risk detection.

### Negative dynamics examples

- increased escalations;
- unclear responsibility;
- hidden rework;
- more approvals without prioritization;
- higher customer complaints;
- increased cognitive load;
- AI-generated errors appearing later;
- reduced human judgment quality.

### Core question

> Is the business process becoming healthier, or merely faster?

---

## 7. Learning and Improvement Interface

The system must learn from operations.

It must not only store logs.

It must convert experience into improved governance.

It should record:

- approved decisions;
- rejected AI actions;
- corrected AI outputs;
- new risk patterns;
- recurring bottlenecks;
- owner decisions;
- changed boundaries;
- new escalation rules;
- failure patterns;
- improvement recommendations.

### Learning categories

| Memory Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Personal memory | Helps adapt to individual working style |
| Team memory | Preserves shared workflow knowledge |
| Business process memory | Tracks process development and recurring issues |
| Governance memory | Stores boundaries, approvals and corrections |
| Incident memory | Helps prevent repeated failures |
| Strategic memory | Tracks owner-level decisions and development direction |

## What the System Should Separate

The prototype must also separate important things that should not collapse into one another.

### 1. Analysis and execution

AI may analyze without being allowed to act.

### 2. Recommendation and decision

AI may recommend, but accountable humans decide.

### 3. Drafting and approval

AI may draft, but approval must remain explicit where risk requires it.

### 4. Personalization and delegated identity

A personalized AI assistant is not automatically allowed to represent a person operationally.

### 5. Technical success and business health

A workflow may become faster but less governable.

### 6. Autonomy and accountability

AI autonomy may increase, but accountability must remain human and organizational.

## Business Owner Dashboard Requirements

The owner interface should provide a decision-level summary.

### Required sections

#### 1. Business Process Health

A simple classification:

- improving;
- stable;
- deteriorating;
- under review;
- requires intervention.

#### 2. Positive Dynamics

What is getting better and why.

#### 3. Negative Dynamics

What is getting worse and where correction is needed.

#### 4. Development Recommendations

Where to invest, expand, automate or improve.

#### 5. Sanitation Recommendations

Where to stop, reduce, restructure or impose stricter controls.

#### 6. Strategic Decisions Waiting

Decisions that cannot be delegated to AI or middle management.

#### 7. Governance Load

How much human approval, escalation and review the system currently requires.

#### 8. Value and Risk Balance

A compact comparison of gained value and accumulated risk.

- AI may classify tickets.
- AI may retrieve policy.

- AI may suggest refund options.

### Owner view

The owner sees:

- response time trend;
- customer satisfaction trend;
- escalation trend;
- cost trend;
- governance load;
- recommendations for development;
- recommendations for correction or sanitation.

## Minimum Viable Prototype

The first version does not need real autonomous agents.

It can use simulated data.

The purpose of the first version is to demonstrate the interface logic:

- how business owners see development and sanitation decisions;
- how managers see coordination;
- how governance owners see boundaries;
- how AI action requests move through the gateway;
- how process dynamics become visible;
- how learning updates future governance.

---

## What This Prototype Is Not

It is not:

- a generic chatbot UI;
- a normal analytics dashboard;
- an LLMOps dashboard only;
- a task manager only;
- an agent orchestration panel only;
- a static governance checklist.

It is a coordination interface for responsibility-aware autonomous operations.

---

## Strategic Formula

> The future interface is not where humans merely command AI.
>
> It is where humans, AI agents and organizations preserve shared responsibility under autonomy.

---

## Closing Principle

The interface must evolve faster than autonomy.

If agent autonomy increases faster than the human coordination interface, the organization risks losing visibility, judgment and accountability.

Therefore, every new AI capability should trigger an interface question:

> What must become visible, governable and decision-ready for humans before this autonomy is expanded?
