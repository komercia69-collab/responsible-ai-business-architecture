## LinkedIn Post — Layer 6 Diagram

Most AI architectures already define:

```text
model
runtime
memory
tools / extensions
routing
```

But when AI begins to act inside real business workflows, one more layer becomes necessary:

```text
governance of responsibility
```

That is what I am exploring with Responsible AI Business Architecture (RABA).

The core idea is simple:

> AI may be probabilistic. Responsibility must not be.

The question is not only whether AI can draft, recommend, route, read context or call tools.

The harder question is:

```text
Who authorized the action?
Under which boundary?
At what approval state?
What was logged?
Who owns the outcome?
What happens when the case should escalate?
```

RABA calls this the missing governance layer — Layer 6 above model, runtime, memory, tools and routing.

It does not replace the AI stack.

It makes responsibility visible when AI-supported output moves toward business action.

I am currently seeking critical feedback on this architecture from practitioners in AI platforms, governance, risk, compliance, security and agentic workflows.

GitHub: https://github.com/komercia69-collab/responsible-ai-business-architecture

I would be grateful for critical feedback, especially from people working with AI agents, LLM gateways, MCP, auditability, SIEM / DFIR, regulated workflows or responsible automation.
