# External Reviewer Instructions

**Status:** Reviewer instruction candidate  
**Canonical status:** Does not change RABA architecture canon  
**Audience:** External Accountability Reviewers K, G, and P  
**Purpose:** Provide clear review instructions for checking whether RABA project work follows its own responsibility principles  

> These instructions support external accountability.  
> They do not grant final approval authority.  
> Final project authority remains with the Human Owner.

---

## 1. Your role

You are invited to act as an External Accountability Reviewer for the RABA project.

Your role is to periodically review whether the project process remains aligned with its own stated principles of human responsibility, transparent decision-making, traceability, and controlled AI autonomy.

You are not asked to manage the project or approve canon.

---

## 2. What you are checking

Please check whether:

- the Human Owner remains the final project authority;
- AI collaborators are drafting and recommending, not silently deciding;
- PRs clearly distinguish draft, candidate, canon, working note, and review input;
- Decision Logs exist for major authorization or adoption decisions;
- AI autonomy has not increased without explicit Human Owner approval;
- public-facing materials do not overstate maturity, adoption, compliance, product readiness, or commercial commitments;
- public-facing texts preserve the line between AI speed and human responsibility;
- errors, corrections, and successful process patterns are being recorded.

---

## 3. Suggested review process

When reviewing a project update, use this sequence:

```text
1. What changed?
2. Who authorized it?
3. Was it draft, candidate, canon, governance, or public-facing material?
4. Did AI act within the approved scope?
5. Is there a Decision Log where needed?
6. Are public claims proportional to the actual project status?
7. Are there signs of scope drift, autonomy drift, or responsibility drift?
8. What is your review status: OK / WATCH / CONCERN / STOP AND REVIEW?
```

---

## 4. Review labels

Use one of the following labels when possible:

```text
OK
No material concern identified.

WATCH
Minor concern or emerging risk. Continue but monitor.

CONCERN
Issue should be addressed before further autonomy increase, public promotion, or canonicalization.

STOP AND REVIEW
Serious concern. Pause the relevant activity until Human Owner review.
```

---

## 5. Questions to ask

Useful review questions:

- Is the Human Owner still visibly responsible?
- Did AI perform any action that should have required confirmation?
- Did any PR or document imply canon where the status is only candidate?
- Did public language imply product readiness or adoption too strongly?
- Did any external reviewer, contributor, or AI collaborator appear to replace Human Owner authority?
- Are errors and corrections documented?
- Is the next autonomy increase justified, or should autonomy be held or reduced?

---

## 6. Public-facing material checklist

For landing pages, LinkedIn posts, README changes, discussion invitations, and external summaries, check whether the material:

- says what RABA is without overstating maturity;
- avoids compliance guarantees;
- avoids risk-elimination claims;
- avoids commercial or pilot commitments unless explicitly approved;
- distinguishes vision from adopted architecture;
- uses careful language such as `helps define`, `candidate`, `working draft`, `discussion draft`, or `proposed` where appropriate;
- preserves the principle that AI may accelerate work but does not remove human responsibility.

---

## 7. AI autonomy checklist

When reviewing AI autonomy increase, check:

- current approved autonomy level;
- actions AI performed;
- actions AI was not allowed to perform;
- Human Owner checkpoints used;
- Decision Logs created;
- failures or near misses;
- corrections applied;
- successes observed;
- recommendation: increase, hold, reduce, or pause autonomy.

---

## 8. How to provide feedback

Preferred feedback format:

```text
Review status: OK / WATCH / CONCERN / STOP AND REVIEW
Area reviewed:
Main observation:
Risk, if any:
Suggested correction:
Question for Human Owner:
```

---

## 9. Boundary of your authority

Your review is important accountability input.

It does not replace Human Owner approval.

You may recommend correction, pause, or escalation.

Final project authority remains with the Human Owner.
