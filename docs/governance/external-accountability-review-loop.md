**Status:** Repository governance / accountability review candidate

**Purpose:** Provide external observation and challenge for the RABA project process, AI autonomy increase, public-facing materials, and adherence to RABA principles  
**Human Owner:** Oleksandr / Александр  

> External review supports accountability.  
> It does not replace Human Owner authority.  
> This document does not change RABA architecture canon, repository permissions, or final approval rights.

---

## 1. Why this exists

RABA is being developed with AI assistance, increasing operational autonomy, public-facing materials, and multiple contributors or reviewers.

That creates a responsibility challenge:

```text
The project must not violate its own principles while developing those principles.
```

The External Accountability Review Loop provides a structured way for trusted external colleagues to periodically review whether the project process remains aligned with RABA principles.

## 3. Core rule

```text
External review increases accountability, but does not replace Human Owner authority.
```

Reviewers may identify concerns, ask for clarification, recommend correction, or flag process drift.

They may not:

- adopt RABA canon;
- merge PRs as final authority;
- approve repository governance changes;
- change access permissions;
- approve public positioning as final authority;
- authorize pilot, vendor, referral, or commercial commitments;
- replace the Human Owner's final responsibility.

---

## 4. What external reviewers should check

External reviewers should periodically check whether:

- AI autonomy increased without explicit Human Owner decision;
- AI performed actions outside approved scope;
- draft, candidate, canonical, working-note, and external-signal statuses are clearly distinguished;
- PRs were opened, reviewed, or merged according to repository governance rules;
- Decision Logs distinguish authorization to prepare work from final adoption;
- public-facing materials overstate RABA maturity, adoption, product readiness, compliance guarantees, or commercial commitments;
- landing pages, LinkedIn posts, GitHub README changes, and discussion invitations remain aligned with current project status;
- contributors, reviewers, and AI collaborators are not treated as final approval authorities;
- errors, near misses, corrections, and successful governance patterns are recorded;
- Human Owner responsibility remains visible and explicit.

---

## 5. Review cadence

Recommended cadence:

- after major PRs;
- before canonicalization decisions;
- before public-facing promotion;
- after any AI autonomy increase;
- monthly during active development;
- ad hoc when the Human Owner requests review.

---

## 6. Inputs for review

External reviewers may inspect:

- active PRs;
- merged PRs;
- Decision Logs;
- governance documents;
- autonomy status reports;
- project accountability reports;
- public-facing materials;
- LinkedIn posts or comments used for positioning;
- landing pages and discussion pages;
- issue threads and review comments.

---

## 7. Expected outputs

External reviewers may provide:

- concerns;
- questions;
- objections;
- suggested corrections;
- requests for clarification;
- status labels such as `ok`, `watch`, `concern`, or `stop and review`.

Suggested review labels:

```text
OK — no material concern identified.
WATCH — minor concern or emerging risk.
CONCERN — issue should be addressed before further autonomy increase or public promotion.
STOP AND REVIEW — serious concern; pause relevant activity until Human Owner review.
```

---

## 8. Public-facing materials review

Public-facing materials include:

- landing pages;
- LinkedIn posts and comments;
- GitHub README changes;
- discussion invitations;
- external summaries;
- pilot or partner-facing language;
- presentations or demos.

Reviewers should check that these materials do not:

- present RABA as more mature than it is;
- imply certification, compliance guarantee, or risk elimination;
- imply institutional adoption where none exists;
- imply product readiness where only a draft or candidate exists;
- create pilot, vendor, referral, or commercial commitments;
- blur the distinction between vision, candidate, canon, and implementation.

---

## 9. AI autonomy review

When AI autonomy is increased, reviewers should check:

- what autonomy level was used;
- what actions AI performed;
- which actions required Human Owner confirmation;
- whether AI stayed inside approved scope;
- whether Decision Logs were created when needed;
- whether failures and corrections were recorded;
- whether the next autonomy increase is justified, should be held, or should be reduced.

---

## 10. Non-authority boundary

External accountability reviewers provide review input.

They do not receive final project authority through this loop.

Final project authority remains with the Human Owner.
