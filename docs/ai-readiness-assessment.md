# AI Readiness Assessment

## Responsible AI Business Architecture

> AI integration is not a plugin installation.
> It is a socio-technical architecture change.

---

## Purpose

This framework helps organizations evaluate whether they are operationally, technically, and organizationally prepared for AI adoption.

The objective is not maximum automation.

The objective is sustainable and governable AI integration.

---

# The Five Readiness Domains

## 1. Process Maturity

### Questions

- Are business processes documented?
- Are workflows standardized?
- Are responsibilities clearly assigned?
- Are operational metrics already tracked?
- Are escalation paths defined?

### Risk

Unstructured organizations often automate chaos instead of improving operations.

### Readiness Scale

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Processes undocumented |
| 2 | Partial standardization |
| 3 | Stable repeatable workflows |
| 4 | Measured and optimized workflows |
| 5 | Fully governed operational architecture |

---

## 2. Data Quality and Availability

### Questions

- Is business data reliable?
- Is data centralized?
- Are permissions controlled?
- Is sensitive data classified?
- Are audit logs available?

### Risk

AI systems amplify poor-quality data.

### Readiness Scale

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Fragmented unreliable data |
| 2 | Partial visibility |
| 3 | Structured operational data |
| 4 | Governed and monitored data |
| 5 | Enterprise-grade data governance |

---

## 3. Technical Infrastructure

### Questions

- Are systems integrated?
- Are APIs available?
- Is identity and access management implemented?
- Is monitoring active?
- Are rollback and recovery procedures defined?

### Risk

Weak infrastructure creates uncontrolled automation risk.

### Readiness Scale

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Manual disconnected systems |
| 2 | Limited integration |
| 3 | Stable operational systems |
| 4 | Monitored scalable architecture |
| 5 | Resilient automation-ready infrastructure |

---

## 4. Governance and Risk Management

### Questions

- Are AI permissions defined?
- Are human approval gates implemented?
- Are escalation procedures documented?
- Are high-risk actions identified?
- Is there an emergency stop protocol?

### Risk

Autonomous systems without governance become organizational liabilities.

### Readiness Scale

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | No governance |
| 2 | Informal controls |
| 3 | Defined governance policies |
| 4 | Active monitoring and auditing |
| 5 | Mature governance architecture |

---

## 5. Organizational Readiness

### Questions

- Do employees understand AI limitations?
- Is there executive sponsorship?
- Are accountability owners assigned?
- Is AI training available?
- Is resistance management planned?

### Risk

AI adoption fails when organizational trust collapses.

### Readiness Scale

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Fear and confusion |
| 2 | Experimental adoption |
| 3 | Structured adoption support |
| 4 | Cross-functional AI alignment |
| 5 | AI-aware operational culture |

---

# Final Readiness Levels

| Score Range | Readiness Status |
|---|---|
| 5–9 | Not Ready |
| 10–14 | Early Exploration |
| 15–19 | Controlled Pilot Stage |
| 20–24 | Operationally Ready |
| 25 | Enterprise AI Governance Mature |

---

# Strategic Principle

The question is not:

"Can this organization use AI?"

The real question is:

"Can this organization remain controllable after AI integration?"

---

# Recommended Usage

This assessment can be used:

- before AI pilot projects;
- during vendor evaluations;
- during governance audits;
- before enterprise deployment;
- for board-level readiness reviews.
