## Purpose

This document provides a working map of active RABA work areas, review candidates, and related operational tracks.

It is intended to help new visitors understand where the project currently has activity without treating that activity as canon, adoption, validation, endorsement, partnership, or implementation guidance.

This map is a navigation aid only.

This document does not create RABA canon.

It does not approve, adopt, validate, certify, endorse, merge, commercialize, implement, or publicly position any listed concept.

It does not replace Human Owner review.

Repository presence does not mean approval.  
Merged non-canonical text does not become canon automatically.  
External feedback does not mean validation.

## How to read this map

Each work area is listed with a current working status.

Common statuses:

- **Working concept** — actively developed idea, not canon.
- **Working inquiry** — open question or exploratory direction.
- **Working note** — written non-canonical material used for review or development.
- **External signal** — outside input recorded for comparison, not validation.
- **Review aid** — support material for analysis or evaluation.
- **Operational control material** — project-control or workflow coordination material.
- **Canonicalization candidate** — may be reviewed for canon, but is not canon yet.
- **Blocked** — requires Human Owner decision or further review before action.
- **Canon** — only material explicitly approved by the Human Owner and marked as canon.

## Related high-attention areas

These areas require particular care because they can easily create public-positioning or canon ambiguity.

### Canonicalization candidates

Canonicalization candidates are not canon.

They require Human Owner review before adoption into RABA architecture.

High-attention examples include:

- Tranche 1.1 canonicalization candidate package;
- Responsibility Observability;
- concepts related to responsibility trace and responsibility binding.

### External signals

External signals may be valuable, but they are not validation.

They must not be treated as adoption, endorsement, partnership, provider approval, or commercial interest.

### Provider and capability signals

Provider, model, platform, or capability-frontier signals may inform RABA development.

They do not make RABA vendor-dependent and do not create provider-facing claims unless explicitly approved by the Human Owner.

### Public-facing material

Public-facing clarity is useful, but it must not create the impression that RABA is a finished standard, certified method, validated framework, adoption-ready product, or compliance guarantee.

## Open questions for contributors

Visitors and contributors can help by engaging with questions such as:

- When does human approval become responsible authorization?
- What status should AI-prepared information have before it enters official decision chains?
- What evidence should be required before AI-prepared summaries become decision inputs?
- How should an AI-agent action be stopped, limited, deferred, escalated, or released?
- What should count as a responsibility trace?
- How should organizations detect human-in-the-loop theater?
- When does multi-agent agreement create false confidence?
- How should external signals be used without becoming false validation?
- What must humans learn before using AI agents responsibly?

## Safe ways to contribute

Useful contributions include:

- asking a governance question;
- identifying unclear wording;
- challenging a concept;
- suggesting a real use case;
- proposing a responsibility failure scenario;
- reviewing whether a status label is clear;
- sharing an external signal for private comparison;
- explaining how a concept appears from a specific professional role.

Useful feedback does not need to agree with RABA.

A well-formed challenge may be more valuable than agreement.
