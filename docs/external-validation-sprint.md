# External Validation Sprint

This document defines the next project phase for RABA: validating external clarity, relevance and pilot interest.

The goal is not to add a new core concept.

The goal is to test whether external readers can understand the problem, the Layer 6 framing, the minimal pilot shape and the practical value of RABA.

---

## Purpose

RABA now has enough internal architecture, examples and pilot-readiness material to begin external validation.

The next question is not:

```text
Can we add more architecture?
```

The next question is:

```text
Do external CTOs, platform teams, risk / compliance people and AI practitioners understand the problem and see a useful pilot path?
```

---

## Sprint Goal

Validate whether external people understand the following within 5–10 minutes:

```text
1. What problem RABA addresses.
2. Why AI action requires governance beyond model/runtime/tooling layers.
3. What Layer 6 means.
4. How the RABA state model works.
5. What a minimal pilot could look like.
```

---

## Target Audiences

Initial validation should focus on:

- CTOs;
- platform engineering leads;
- AI product owners;
- risk / compliance stakeholders;
- security / DFIR / SIEM practitioners;
- people working with agentic workflows, LLM gateways, MCP or AI automation.

---

## Validation Channels

Recommended first channels:

1. LinkedIn post using the Layer 6 diagram.
2. Direct LinkedIn messages to selected contacts.
3. GitHub repository link with a recommended reading path.
4. Short pilot-intake questions for people who show interest.

## Validation Questions

When talking to external readers, ask:

```text
1. Is the problem understandable?
2. Does “Layer 6” make sense as a positioning frame?
3. Which part feels strongest?
4. Which part feels too abstract?
5. Would this help in a real workflow?
6. What would make the first pilot easier to say yes to?
7. Which role in your organization would care most: CTO, platform, product, risk, compliance, security, operations?
```

---

## Success Signals

Strong signals:

- a reader can explain RABA back in their own words;
- someone identifies a real workflow where AI output may become business action;
- a CTO or platform lead asks how it would integrate;
- a risk / compliance stakeholder asks what the Decision Log contains;
- someone agrees to a short follow-up conversation;
- someone suggests a concrete pilot workflow.

Weak signals:

- readers understand the principle but not the implementation path;
- people see it as a manifesto rather than a practical layer;
- Layer 6 sounds interesting but not actionable;
- pilot scope feels too broad.

---

## Sprint Outputs

The sprint should produce:

```text
- external feedback notes;
- list of interested contacts;
- unclear terms or objections;
- revised LinkedIn / outreach language;
- refined pilot intake questions;
- one or more candidate pilot workflows.
```

---

## Scope Discipline

During this sprint, avoid adding new large architecture concepts unless external feedback proves that a gap blocks understanding or pilot readiness.

Preferred work:

```text
clarify
simplify
show examples
improve reading path
collect objections
identify pilot workflows
```

Avoid:

```text
new conceptual branches
new large frameworks
unvalidated implementation expansion
premature enterprise claims
```

---

## Core Reminder

External validation is not a sales campaign.

It is a learning sprint.

The goal is to find out whether RABA is understandable, relevant and pilotable from the perspective of people who did not participate in building it.
