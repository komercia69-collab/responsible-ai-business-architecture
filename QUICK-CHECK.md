# AI Readiness Quick Check

Answer 7 questions before starting your first AI pilot.

This is a fast 3-minute self-check for organizations, teams, consultants, and business owners who want to use AI without losing control, responsibility, auditability, or operational stability.

> AI may be probabilistic. Responsibility must not be.

## Why This Exists

Many AI initiatives begin with a tool, a vendor, or a general promise of automation.

This framework starts with a different question:

> Is this business process ready for a responsible AI pilot?

The Quick Check helps you see whether one concrete AI idea is a promising pilot candidate, whether the process needs more preparation, or whether it is too risky as a first step.

## How to Use It

Choose one concrete business process that you may want to improve with AI.

Examples:

- customer email triage;
- invoice processing;
- internal knowledge search;
- support ticket classification;
- reporting drafts;
- document review;
- service request routing.

Score each question:

- 0 = no / unclear
- 1 = partly
- 2 = yes / clear

## Interpretation

### 12–14 Points — Good Pilot Candidate

This process may be suitable for a first controlled AI pilot.

Recommended next step:

- define the AI role;
- avoid autonomous execution at the beginning;
- define data and permission boundaries;
- add a Confirmation Gate where needed;
- define simple success metrics;
- run a limited pilot.

### 7–11 Points — Needs Preparation

The process may have AI potential, but the organization should prepare before implementation.

Recommended next step:

- clarify process ownership;
- map the current workflow;
- identify data and risk points;
- define human review;
- avoid giving AI broad permissions;
- choose a smaller pilot scope if needed.

### 0–6 Points — Not Ready Yet

This process is probably not ready for AI integration as a first pilot.

Recommended next step:

- understand the process better;
- reduce operational chaos;
- define responsibility;
- improve data quality;
- choose a lower-risk process;
- avoid full automation.

## Immediate Red Flags

Be careful if any of these are true:

- nobody owns the process;
- the process affects payments, employment, legal rights, safety, or sensitive personal data;
- AI would need broad access to confidential systems;
- employees would be expected to approve AI output without enough context;
- management expects full automation immediately;
- mistakes would be hard to detect, reverse, or explain.

If several red flags are present, do not start with autonomous AI.

## What This Quick Check Gives You

In a few minutes, you should know:

1. Whether your AI idea is a reasonable pilot candidate.
2. Whether the process needs preparation before automation.
3. Where responsibility and human control need attention.
4. Whether a discovery session, pilot canvas, or decision report should follow.

## Important Boundary

This is not legal advice, compliance certification, security certification, or a guarantee that AI should be implemented.

It is a practical first diagnostic tool for deciding whether a business process deserves deeper review.

## Key Statement

> Do not ask only what AI can do.  
> Ask what AI should be allowed to do, under whose responsibility, with which controls, and for which business value.
