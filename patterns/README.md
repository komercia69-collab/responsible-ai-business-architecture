# Governance Failure Patterns

AI systems rarely fail only because of model quality.

Many AI pilot failures emerge from weak socio-technical governance structures, unclear responsibility boundaries, invisible AI influence, and missing operational controls.

This section documents recurring governance and operational failure patterns that organizations may encounter during AI adoption and AI-assisted decision-making.

---

## Purpose

The goal of these patterns is to help organizations:

- identify governance weaknesses early;
- recognize hidden operational risks;
- improve escalation structures;
- strengthen responsibility continuity;
- design observable AI-assisted workflows.

---

## Why This Matters

AI failures are often framed as technical failures.

In practice, many failures originate from:

- weak responsibility architecture;
- unclear escalation paths;
- recommendation/execution ambiguity;
- invisible automation expansion;
- governance drift over time.

Understanding governance failure patterns helps organizations design more resilient AI operating models.
