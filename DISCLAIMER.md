# Disclaimer

This project is an early-stage conceptual and practical framework for responsible AI integration into business processes.

It is intended for education, research, discussion, architecture thinking, consulting preparation, and structured feedback.

## Not Legal Advice

The materials in this repository do not constitute legal advice.

They do not replace professional legal review, GDPR assessment, EU AI Act assessment, data protection impact assessment, compliance audit, or regulatory consultation.

Organizations should consult qualified legal, compliance, data protection, and risk professionals before implementing AI systems in real business processes.

## Not a Certification Standard

This framework is not an official standard, certification, or regulatory compliance guarantee.

Using the materials in this repository does not automatically make an AI system compliant with GDPR, the EU AI Act, industry regulations, contractual obligations, or internal governance requirements.

## No Implementation Guarantee

The project provides concepts, patterns, questions, and templates.

It does not guarantee that any specific AI implementation will be safe, compliant, profitable, secure, or technically reliable.

Every real-world implementation must be evaluated in its own context, including:

- business process design
- data sensitivity
- legal obligations
- technical architecture
- security controls
- human oversight
- audit requirements
- organizational responsibility
- operational risk

## Human Responsibility

AI systems may support, prepare, summarize, classify, recommend, or automate certain tasks.

However, organizations remain responsible for how AI systems are selected, configured, deployed, monitored, and used.

Responsibility cannot be delegated to an AI system.

## Early-Stage Status

This project is under active development.

Terminology, structure, patterns, and recommendations may change as feedback is collected from practitioners, researchers, architects, consultants, legal professionals, and business users.

## Use With Care

The materials should be used as a starting point for structured thinking, not as a final implementation checklist.

Before applying this framework in a real organization, review it with appropriate professionals and adapt it to the specific legal, technical, and business context.
