# Contributing

Thank you for your interest in contributing to Responsible AI Business Architecture.

This project is in an early conceptual and practical stage. Contributions are welcome, especially when they help make responsible AI integration more practical, understandable, auditable, and useful for real organizations.

## What Kind of Contributions Are Useful?

Useful contributions may include:

- feedback on the framework structure;
- practical business process examples;
- new architecture patterns;
- risk and compliance improvements;
- clearer terminology;
- diagrams and visual explanations;
- case studies;
- consulting templates;
- review from CTOs, DPOs, architects, consultants, founders, or business process owners.

## Contribution Principles

### 1. Preserve Human Responsibility

The framework is based on the principle:

> AI may be probabilistic. Responsibility must not be.

Contributions should support clear human responsibility, not blur it.

### 2. Start From Business Processes

Do not begin with AI tools, vendors, or models.

Begin with:

- business process;
- data;
- decisions;
- responsibility;
- risk;
- human control;
- auditability.

### 3. Be Practical

A useful contribution should help someone apply the framework in a real organization.

Prefer concrete examples, templates, questions, patterns, and decision criteria over abstract claims.

### 4. Avoid Overclaiming

Do not present the framework as a guarantee of legal, technical, financial, or regulatory safety.

Use careful language, especially around GDPR, the EU AI Act, compliance, employment, health, safety, finance, and legal decision-making.

## How to Contribute

### Option 1 — Open an Issue

Use an issue when you want to:

- suggest an improvement;
- report unclear wording;
- propose a new document;
- ask a structured question;
- identify a risk or missing topic.

### Option 2 — Join a Discussion

Use GitHub Discussions for broader questions, early ideas, and feedback.

Current open discussion:

[How can organizations integrate AI responsibly without losing control?](https://github.com/komercia69-collab/responsible-ai-business-architecture/discussions/2)

### Option 3 — Submit a Pull Request

Use a pull request when you want to propose a concrete change to files in the repository.

A good pull request should:

- have a clear title;
- explain the reason for the change;
- keep the framework style consistent;
- avoid unnecessary complexity;
- preserve attribution and transparency.

## Suggested Formats

### New Architecture Pattern

Use this structure when proposing a new pattern:

```markdown
# Pattern Name

## Core Idea

## Problem

## When to Use

## When Not to Use

## Basic Flow

## Human Responsibility

## Audit Requirements

## Risks and Anti-Patterns

## Practical Example
```

### New Practical Example

Use this structure when proposing a business case:

```markdown
# Example: Name of AI Pilot

## Business Context

## Current Problems

## AI Opportunity

## AI Role

## Data Sensitivity

## Decision Risk

## Required Human Control

## Audit Requirements

## Responsibility Model

## Pilot Scope

## Success Metrics

## Red Flags

## First Pilot Recommendation
```

## Authorship and Attribution

The original concept was initiated by Oleksandr Shuliak.

Meaningful contributions should be documented through commits, issues, discussions, or pull requests so that the development history remains transparent.

Contributors may be added to [AUTHORS.md](AUTHORS.md) when they provide substantial improvements, examples, legal analysis, architecture patterns, diagrams, implementation templates, or practical case studies.

## Style Guide

Please write in clear professional English.

Prefer:

- short paragraphs;
- practical examples;
- explicit risk language;
- clear distinction between AI support, recommendation, preparation, and execution;
- human responsibility and auditability.

Avoid:

- hype language;
- promises of full automation without controls;
- unsupported legal claims;
- vague ethical statements without operational meaning;
- vendor-specific promotion.

## Key Statement

> Responsible AI integration begins before implementation.

It begins when an organization understands its processes, responsibilities, risks, and control boundaries.
